FoodCulture

Mobile Case Study

THE PROBLEM: Consumers are wanting more insight and authentic reviews for restaurants close to them. They would also like the ability to order and have a fast delivery service.

THE SOLUTION: The Food Culture app will allow users to search for specific food places around their location and be able to see accurate reviews from people who have dined there previously. In addition, the app also offers order and delivery/pick up services.

OUR ROLE: UX/UI Designer

TOOLS: Miro, Figma, Excel, Google Slides.

Clickable Prototype

The FoodCulture app will help users to search for local options that are categorised by culture and food type to make it easier for them to explore their local options. There will also be review sections on each restaurant so that users can see how others enjoyed their meals or leave a review themselves.

Findings

Collaboration This is the very first case study where we work in a group. Since we have only done individual projects, it was a learning experience to brainstorm, split work, and provide feedback amongst each other. We all live in different time zones with a three hour difference, so it was challenging at times to meet up and discuss work. Through this experience, we learned how to efficiently collaborate with each other.

Designing Features Throughout this case study, we had many ideas of what features to add to our mobile app. Although it was exciting to come up with more features, it would sometimes leave us feeling a little overwhelmed, especially during the prototyping stage. We learned how to prioritize features that would help us solve our problem statement.

Iteration Opportunities We received many valuable feedback from our users during testing. It was challenging to narrow down all these significant findings and figure out how to efficiently iterate our prototype. Through this experience, we learned what additional features to add to our app based on user findings.
